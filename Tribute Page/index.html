Dr. Neil deGrasse Tyson

The hero humanity needs
Here is a timeline of Dr. Tyson's life:
  • 1958: born and raised in New York City, NY
  • 1976: graduated from the Bronx High School of Science
  • 1980: earned a BA in Physics from Harvard University
  • 1983: earned a master's degree in Astronomy from the University of Texas at Austin
  • 1989: earned a master's degree in Astrophysics from Columbia University
  • 1991: graduated from Columbia University with a PhD in Astrophysics
  • 1995: first occupant of the Fredrick P. Rose Directorship of the Hayden Planetarium
  • 2000: voted Sexiest Astrophysicist Alive by People Magazine
  • 2001: appinted by President Bush to serve on a 12-member commission to study the Future of the US Aerospace Industry
  • 2002: final report was published, which recommended promotion of a thriving future of transportation, space exploration, and national security
  • 2004: appointed by President Bush to serve a 9-member commission, named the Moon, Mars and Beyond, on the Implementation of the US Space Exploration Policy, which navigated a path by which the new space vision can become a successful part of the American agenda
  • 2004: recipient of the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal
  • 2006: appointed by the head of NASA to serve on its Advistory Council
  • 2006: became the on-camera host of PBS-NOVA's spinoff program NOVA ScienceNow; an accessible look at the frontier of all the science that shapes the unerstanding of our places in the universe
  • Receipient of nine honorary doctorates
  • Recognized by the International Astronomical Union in their official naming of asteroid 13123 Tyson, due to his contributions to the public appreciation of the cosmos
  • His new venture is Start Talk Radio, a popular commercial radio program devoted to all things space
